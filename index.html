What is climate change?

The atmosphere naturally traps some heat from the sun (the greenhouse effect), which keeps Earth livable. But adding extra greenhouse gases like carbon dioxide and methane makes that heat blanket thicker, raising global temperatures and disrupting weather systems.

Main causes

  • Burning coal, oil, and gas for energy
  • Deforestation and land-use change
  • Industrial agriculture and livestock emissions

Why scientists are confident

  • Global thermometers and ocean heat records
  • Ice-core and satellite observations
  • Independent climate models reaching similar conclusions

Why it matters

  • More extreme heat, floods, droughts, and wildfires
  • Strain on food, water, and health systems
  • Rising risks for coastal communities and biodiversity
